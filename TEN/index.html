Week Ten

We had a shorter class this week which only left us with half of our usual time (3 hours). With the time that I had it was important for me to debug my project that I had difficulties with. I was able to get assistance from Karen where we managed to revise the entire code and get the spheres to spawn and move around the page.

During the week I also continued coding my SKO where I started coding a button that played audio when pressed. I created a spotify playlist a few weeks ago that provided some inspiration and style of instrumental I wanted for my SKO. The music overall had bouncy rhythms and a playful mood which was really what I had in mind to fit my animated gifs. I also had a look through the Freesound website to collect additional instrumentals that I could possibly use for my website, I managed to find a few that were in the style I was aiming for and that could loop.

From this idea I found a p5.js sketch on how to create a play and pause button and coded it to my website and I chose the audio Rocktronica by Tsundere Twintails to test it. The button was able to play, pause and resume where it had paused, but I wasn’t sure how to keep looping the audio.

Instead of dwelling on how to loop the audio I just moved on to the placement and customisation of the button. I played around with the its position and started of with placing it on the top left of the screen but then I decided to place it in the center of the page above the dance party as it made the webpage look more balanced. I also then changed the colour, sizing and words written in the button.

References followed:

17.2: Play and Pause Button - p5.js Sound Tutorialby Ruyi_Chen

I also started thinking about how to structure my reflection onto the website and planned to have the text and images scroll/slide out at the bottom of the dance party when the weeks are clicked instead of opening separate pages. I also learnt the code of createA() where it creates a link to another page on p5.js. I’m not sure if it can be used to show text on the same page, but it could be something useful to know.

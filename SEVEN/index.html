Week Seven

This week we first had time as a class to share the paper prototypes we created and express any adjustments or changes made. In smaller groups I was able to listen about the works that others have created and how they have communicated their messages through features on their web pages. Although I haven’t made any major changes to my paper prototype I anticipate that some refinements will be added when I actually get to coding to see what works and what doesn’t.

Afterwards, we had a discussion on the use of VR and AR and how this design medium is changing the digital space to create immersive artworks within online environments. We were able to observe a project called “The Blended Self” where the interactive 3D space was made in a week using the programs Blender and Unity. A useful source that was also obtained from the discussion called “STYLY Gallery” provides an online community to share and explore works of AR and VR for free.

We then reviewed our plans for the design of our SKO websites, where I developed a rough structure of my ideas during the mid-semester break in the previous week. For my website I plan on making something that brings a joyful experience. I intend to do this by creating little animated gifs that dance on the webpage while music plays in the background.

I first drew and animated a house character that bounces around and is applied to a starting page. The starting page can then be clicked to switch to a landing page where the links/buttons to each week are arranged in the shape of a roof of a house. I then created more gifs of a cat and dog that dance under the roof and plan on adding additional characters to create a dance party while music will be able to play in the background of the landing page. For the links to each week I plan to either create new pages that open in a new tab or have the pages display in the centre where it replaces the dancing animals.

I was able to code my initial plans on p5.js where I utilised a range of sources to refresh and learn new knowledge on the program. A challenge I faced was getting the gifs on the webpages as I wasn’t sure if there would be a different code for normal images and gifs, however I found out that the code ‘loadImage’ can also import gif files. But from this the gif animation did not display on the screen and was always stuck on loading. I tried finding out why it wasn’t working and found a reddit post that had the exact same problem, where it was because the gif wasn’t specified in a certain folder. Thankfully it was able to load after I identified the folder it was in.

I created the arching roof by fiddling around with the position of each text and experimenting with the spacing to make it even. I really like how my ideas have turned out and I really enjoyed the process of creating the pages and learning new areas of code. I am excited to see how this SKO website will evolve to create an effective experience to showcase my findings throughout the course.

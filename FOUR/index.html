Week Four

This week’s class prepared us for what content we could use for our paper prototype in relation to digital culture.

The list of readings that were offered included:

  • Cyberfeminist Manifesto for the 21st Century
  • Glitch Studies Manifesto
  • Guerilla Open Access Manifesto
  • Lowtech Manifesto
  • Manifeste du Web indépendant
  • Manifesto for the Unstable Media
  • Piracy Manifesto Miltos Manetas
  • The ABC of Tactical Media
  • The Cult of Done Manifesto
  • The Hackers Manifesto
  • The Lo-Fi Manifesto
  • Donna Haraway's Cyborg Manifesto

After browsing through these readings I was still drawn towards the passages from the novel “Age of Earthquakes: A Guide to The Extreme Present” and its philosophy of the current evolution of digital media and technology. From the previous week, I really enjoyed flipping through the novel as it offered a lot of visual elements for an easy read. I was able to find a few pages that I liked and eventually was inspired to create my paper prototype from one of the passages that reads:

“Why would you want to disconnect from the internet?

It's fun.

It keeps you from feeling alone.

It gives you information and doesn't judge you."

The passage highlights the superficial enjoyment that the internet offers, and the false sensation of connection. These enticing aspects that many fail to realise encourages a distraction and addiction to remain constant on the internet for connection. I chose this passage as I feel that it strongly resonates with many lives in the modern day today, where people are constantly online. I then decided to use the first line of the passage as the main text and basis to generate ideas for my prototype.

In the afternoon, the second half of the class continued with more tutorials on how to code on p5.js. I was able to learn about how to work with mouse events and create sketches that are dependent on the position of the mouse or when it is clicked.
